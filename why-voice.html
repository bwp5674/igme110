Why Voice?

Before getting into voice applications, let’s take a look at how the smartphone impacted the internet as we know it. Suddenly, a desktop computer or laptop was no longer necessary as most things could be done with the devices in our pockets, especially with the introduction of mobile-friendly websites and more importantly: apps. All of a sudden, there was an app for everything. Indie developers were making millions of dollars because they were one of the first to create a mobile app for something. Many ideas had already existed within the ecosystem of the internet, but were not adapted for smartphones.

Fast forward to today, a lot of concepts are being thrown around in anticipation for the next groundbreaking interface: VR, AR, blockchain, cloud computing, and IoT to name a few. While voice-user interfaces isn’t exactly a household phrase, the applications are spreading like wildfire. What are they? “Voice user interfaces (VUIs) allow the user to interact with a system through voice or speech commands”. Think of Siri, Alexa, and the Google Assistant. Technology giants are investing billions into their voice ecosystems:

  • This January, Amazon announced that they have sold 100 million Alexa devices.
  • Google announced that the Google Assistant would be installed on one billion devices.
  • Amazon, Microsoft, Google, Apple, Spotify, and Samsung are hard at work at creating their own voice assistants, as well as creating “smart-speakers” with their assistants installed.
  • Amazon was practically giving away Echo Dots like candy last Black Friday in order to get more users on their new platform.

Voice interfaces will not only impact consumers, however. It can be argued that applications for voice interfaces lie in almost every industry, just as the internet and IoT devices changed jobs in almost every field.

Right now, there are some obstacles preventing a voice interfaces from being these game-changing platforms. As developers, we have the ability to accelerate this process by building “voice-optimized” apps for these assistants. There is currently a huge market for third-party, voice-first applications.

While voice interfaces won’t be taking the place of smartphones any time soon, it is important to focus on creating mobile-optimized experiences when developing a smart assistant app (Alexa Skill or Google Assistant Action). Making your service simply run on a smart speaker will not cut it. Instead, build applications optimized for medium. As this occurs, the integration of voice assistants in consumers’ daily lives will become frictionless and your apps will succeed.
